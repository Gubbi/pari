# Who Is Pari For?

## The Champion: Whoever Feels the Drift

The person who reaches for Pari isn't defined by their title or where they sit in an org chart. They're defined by a specific frustration — the feeling that the team's way of working isn't holding, and that no amount of documentation or conversation seems to fix it.

They've experienced one or more of these:

- Writing a team playbook that nobody reads, or that's out of date within a month
- Watching AI agents "go rogue" because they have no context about how this team works
- Onboarding a new member — human or agent — and watching the same context-building happen from scratch, every time
- Having the same alignment conversation repeatedly instead of encoding it once
- Wanting consistency without having to be the one who enforces it manually

That frustration can belong to anyone on a team: someone coordinating work, someone doing the work, someone responsible for the team's output, or someone building agents that participate in it.

They don't want to micromanage. They want to **encode how work happens** — once — and have it actually stick.

## The Daily Users

### Contributors (optionally working through agents)

Often, contributors on a Pari-enabled team interact with Pari indirectly — through their AI agent. The agent checks in with Pari, understands what's expected, and operates accordingly. The contributor benefits from clearer expectations and less re-explaining.

### AI agents

Pari treats AI agents as first-class team members. An agent on a Pari-enabled team knows its role, its accountabilities, what stage of work is active, what's blocking it, and the *why* behind its task. It doesn't start from zero every session.

### Teams building autonomous agents

As teams mature their use of AI, some members start building domain-specific autonomous agents — agents that own entire areas of the workflow. Pari gives those agents the context and guardrails they need to operate within the team's defined process.

## Teams That Get the Most from Pari

**Small teams moving fast**
When it's 3 people and 10 AI agents, there's no time for lengthy onboarding or detailed documentation. Pari lets a small team define their workflow once and have every agent — and every new member — pick it up immediately. In software, this might look like a startup shipping their first product; in other domains, a small research group or a content team operating at pace.

**Larger teams adopting agentic ways of working**
At scale, the cost of alignment failures compounds. An autonomous agent operating without knowledge of the team's process standards, review gates, or ownership model is a liability. Pari gives larger teams the infrastructure to extend their existing way of working to agents safely and consistently.

**Any team where "how we work" matters**
If your team has a methodology — even an informal one — and you want it to actually be followed by both humans and agents, Pari is built for you.

## Where Pari Is Not the Right Fit

**Solo creators without agents**
Pari is built for collaboration — between humans, between humans and agents, between agents. Working entirely alone, without agents, gets little value from a workflow runtime.

**Teams that want a project manager**
Pari doesn't manage tasks, sprints, or roadmaps. If what you need is a better Jira, Pari isn't it.

**Teams where everyone is already aligned**
Pari solves alignment problems. If your team of 3 shares an office, talks all day, and never has misalignment — you might not need this yet.

## A Note on Team Size and Stage

Pari is designed to be useful across very different team contexts:

| Context | How Pari Helps |
|---|---|
| Early-stage startup | Define your workflow early; agents and new hires pick it up immediately |
| Growing startup | Preserve alignment as the team scales without adding process overhead |
| Enterprise team | Bring the team's established workflow to AI agents safely and consistently |
| Any team adopting AI agents | Give agents the context they need to be real participants, not just tools |

The workflow you define in Pari will look different for each of these. That's by design.
