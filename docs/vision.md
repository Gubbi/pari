# Vision

## The World We're Building For

Teams are becoming hybrid. Not eventually — now. Contributors work through AI agents for a significant portion of their daily output. Some team members build autonomous agents that own entire domains. Others use agents to stay informed and make decisions. The ratio of humans to agents on a "team" is already shifting, and it will keep shifting.

This is happening in software development, research, content creation, design, and anywhere that collaborative knowledge work gets done.

Most tooling wasn't built for this world. It was built for humans, with AI bolted on as a feature. Pari is built for hybrid teams from first principles.

## The Core Insight

Teams have always struggled with workflow alignment. The norms of how work gets done — what phases exist, what must be done before what, who is accountable for what — live in people's heads. They're transmitted through onboarding conversations, Slack threads, and tribal memory. They erode as the team changes.

AI agents make this problem acute. An agent has no tribal memory. It has no Slack history. Every session, it starts cold. And unlike a new human hire who will gradually absorb the team's norms over weeks, an agent might be instantiated dozens of times a day across dozens of tasks — each time with no shared context about how this team works.

**Pari's core insight is that this problem is solvable — but not with better prompts.**

Prompts drift. Context windows fill. Agents interpret loosely. The only durable solution is structural: make the team's workflow explicit, machine-readable, and enforced — not suggested.

## What Pari Is

Pari is a **workflow runtime**. Teams define their workflow — the stages, gates, roles, artifacts, and the *why* behind each task and phase. Pari holds that definition and enforces it, deterministically, for everyone operating within it.

Agents don't receive context and hope for the best. They check in with Pari on a loop, asking what they're allowed to do, what's blocking them, and *why* their task matters. Pari answers authoritatively.

## Core Beliefs

**1. Teams know their own workflow best.**
Pari doesn't prescribe a methodology. The team defines how they work — whether that's spec-before-build, prototype-first, research-then-create, or something entirely their own. In software development, this might be SDD, TDD, DDD, or prototype driven. In other domains, the vocabulary will be different. Pari's job is to hold and enforce whatever the team chose, not to have opinions about it.

**2. Enforcement is more durable than suggestion.**
A workflow written in a document doesn't enforce itself. A workflow enforced by Pari does. Gates that actually gate. Sequences that actually sequence. The difference between "we try to review before publishing" and "you cannot publish without a review" is the difference between alignment and drift. In software development: the difference between "we try to spec before coding" and "you cannot start coding without a spec."

**3. Agents are team members, not tools.**
AI agents have roles. They have accountabilities. They can own domains. They need to know the *why* behind their tasks, not just the what. Pari treats agents with the same accountability model as humans — not because they're the same, but because the workflow doesn't need to care about the difference.

**4. The "why" is load-bearing.**
Context-free tasks produce context-free output. When an agent (or a human) understands *why their specific task matters* — not just what to do — they make better decisions at the edges. The why behind a task shapes how it's approached, where judgment calls land, what "done" actually means. That understanding goes deeper when the task sits inside a stage whose purpose is also clear: why this phase exists, why certain work must happen in sequence. Pari stores and delivers that why at every level.

## What Pari Is Not

**Not a project manager.** Pari doesn't replace Linear, Jira, or GitHub Issues. Task tracking, sprint planning, and roadmaps live elsewhere. Pari owns the *how* of work, not the *what*.

**Not a domain standards enforcer.** Pari's concern is the workflow — the stages, gates, and sequences through which work moves. What the work itself looks like is yours to define: how an engineer writes code, how a researcher structures a study, how a content team reviews a draft. Pari holds the process; the craft stays with the team.

**Not prescriptive.** Pari has no preferred methodology. The team that vibes and the team that writes 20-page specs before touching code are both valid users of Pari.

**Not a replacement for trust.** Pari provides structure, not surveillance. The goal is a team — human and agent — that operates within a shared framework they helped define, not a team that feels watched.


## The Long View

The future of knowledge work is hybrid — humans and agents, building together. The teams that thrive in it won't be the ones who found the best prompts or wrote the most detailed playbooks. They'll be the ones who found a durable way to define how they work — and made that understanding available to every participant, in every session.

As the mix of human and agent contributors on a team shifts, the framework for how work happens needs to scale with it. Not by getting more complex, but by becoming more shared. More legible. More alive.

That's the world Pari is building for.
